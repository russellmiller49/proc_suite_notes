**Prompt 12 â€“ Formal dictation style**
```
Patient is a 29-year-old male presenting with airway stenosis. Procedure performed under general anesthesia using a flexible therapeutic bronchoscope. On initial inspection the airway was stable with significant airway pressure and the stent was noted to have migrated distally. Therapeutic aspiration was performed in the proximal middle and distal trachea as well as the right mainstem bronchus intermedius and left mainstem to clear mucus. Bronchoalveolar lavage was performed at the lateral and medial segments of the right middle lobe RB4 and RB5 with 20 cc of normal saline instilled and 10 cc returned. Samples were sent for cell count and microbiology including cultures viral and fungal studies. A BonaStent measuring 14 by 60 was identified as a foreign body in the distal trachea and was removed with forceps. A significant amount of hair was found at the graft level causing endobronchial obstruction with mucus trapping and was mechanically removed with forceps. Granulation tissue was identified in the subglottic region and was treated with argon plasma coagulation using a 1.5 mm probe at pulse 20 effect 2 and flow 0.3 for 2 to 3 second applications. The affected airway improved from 60 percent to 90 percent patency. There was no blood loss and no complications. The patient was extubated in the operating room and transported to recovery in stable condition. Specimens include the BonaStent and BAL from the right middle lobe. Plan is for critical airway watch overnight with consideration of CPAP if respiratory failure develops and follow-up chest x-ray in the morning. Modifier 22 applies to the endobronchial biopsy and excision codes due to the hair removal requiring greater than 40 percent increased work.
```
